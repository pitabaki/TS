Q & A Column System Worthy of Konzerthaus Berlin

February 2015
Typically, column array loudspeakers are not found in leading symphonic halls as most systems aren't known for music and speech clarity. Marking a quantum advancement in delivering exceptional sonic quality and directionality, the CAL column array loudspeaker from Meyer Sound has now been chosen by three world-class classical music venues including Vienna's Musikverein, Amsterdam's Concertgebouw, and Berlin's legendary Konzerthaus.

CAL met the Konzerthaus's needs for architectural friendliness and, most importantly, for extraordinary music and speech reproduction in its Great Hall. In this Q&A, project consultant Ralf Bauer-Diefenbach shares the clarity requirements for the project, his approach to achieving intelligibility, and the selection of CAL.

Q: In concert halls designed for orchestral music, what are the challenges in achieving good speech intelligibility?

Bauer-Diefenbach: Prestigious classical concert halls are designed with architectural features that give both the audience and musicians balanced late reverberations and the important early reflections.
